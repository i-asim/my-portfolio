Having a personal website is more than just an online presence—it's a **living portfolio** that evolves with me. It’s a space where I can showcase my projects, document my growth, and experiment with new ideas. On [iasim.vercel.app](https://iasim.vercel.app), every section is intentional: from the clean design to the modular project cards, it reflects how I approach development—methodical, creative, and client‑centric.

## Why I Built It

I created my personal website for several interconnected reasons:

- **Showcasing My Work** – Instead of scattered GitHub repos or LinkedIn updates, this site acts as a curated hub. Visitors can explore my projects, see the stacks I use (Next.js, WordPress, PHP, MySQL, REST APIs), and understand how I approach problem‑solving.

- **Building My Online Presence** – Social media is temporary; a personal website is permanent. It gives me control over my narrative and branding. The typography, layout, and responsiveness are all deliberate choices to reflect professionalism and clarity.

- **Sharing Knowledge** – Through blog posts, I can write about web development, React, Next.js, and troubleshooting workflows. Each article doubles as documentation for myself and a resource for others navigating similar challenges.

- **Learning & Experimentation** – My site is a sandbox. I test new stacks, play with design systems, and refine performance optimizations here. For example, experimenting with **responsive layouts**, **dynamic content rendering**, and **conversion‑focused engagement** mirrors the kind of work I do professionally.

- **Professional Growth** – Beyond code, this site is a narrative of my journey. It documents milestones, lessons learned, and evolving goals. It’s not just about showcasing skills—it’s about building trust and clarity for collaborators, recruiters, and clients.

## Tech Stack & Design Choices

The website is powered by **Next.js** and deployed on **Vercel** for speed and scalability. I’ve integrated:

- **Responsive Design** – Ensuring accessibility across devices.  
- **Dynamic Content** – Modular project cards and blog posts that can be updated easily.  
- **Conversion‑Focused Layouts** – Clear CTAs and structured navigation for client engagement.  
- **Scalable Architecture** – Repo hygiene and onboarding clarity so collaborators can jump in quickly.  

These choices aren’t just technical—they reflect my character: methodical, proactive, and uncompromising about standards.
